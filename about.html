About


"What do I need to know in order to make a good decision?"

It is a question that I ask myself daily and try to answer through systematic exploration. My approach is to gather information and then take thoughtful action. For example, before I make a purchase on Amazon, be it pet supplies or consumer electronics, I first research articles that publicize beneficial ingredients or latest technical specifications. Then, in addition to comparing ratings and reading customer reviews of similar products, I use price trackers to gauge an item's current price relative to its high point, low point, and cyclical nature. While this may seem like a lot of thought to put into dog shampoo, diligence affords me confidence in determining not only what to buy but also how to buy it, i.e., one-time, on subscription, or in bulk at a later date. In short, I like to make well-informed decisions.

I work for a financial data vendor that services 40 of the world's 50 largest asset management firms, and my role is to keep them engaged with our data management and analytics solutions. I achieve this by following two basic principles: be helpful and be transparent. In support of a data integration team, I may clarify availability, sources, and relationships of data sets. For a business intelligence unit, I may reveal best practices for metrics tracking among industry peers. When assisting a sales enablement group, I may tailor a lead list to their target market. Ultimately, I ensure that clients receive access to relevant data, competitive insights, and analysis-based recommendations.

In my free time, I am learning various programming languages and open-source libraries for data analysis and visualization. I completed UC Berkeley Extension's Data Analytics Boot Camp in June 2020; the curriculum included Python, HTML, Javascript, SQL, and Tableau. Aside from that, I enjoy eclectic cooking, the occasional craft beer, and travel and lifestyle content. I also advocate for good personal finance habits and ecological sustainability.
