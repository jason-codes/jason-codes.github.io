About


My name is Jason. I work for a financial data vendor that services the world’s largest asset management firms, and my role is to keep them engaged with our SaaS applications and analytics solutions. I achieve this by following two basic principles: be helpful and be transparent. Outside of work I dedicate time to learning new programming languages and open-source libraries for data analysis and visualization. As someone who heavily relies on collecting information to drive both personal and professional decision making, I aim to develop pragmatic tools that streamline arduous tasks and promote intelligent action. Whether these projects materialize into web scrapers, interactive dashboards, predictive models, or games of skill, I am thrilled to share them with you here on my personal website and portfolio.

I was born in San Francisco to hardworking immigrants who knew little of the burgeoning prosperity of their adopted hometown but everything about self-sacrifice and family. Every dollar earned at the restaurant or along the delivery route was seemingly spent on their children’s education. My older siblings parlayed our parents’ investment into successful careers in finance and medicine, whereas I turned to fintech after studying economics at the University of California, San Diego. It simply made sense given my number-oriented nature and desire to diversify my skill set. Consequently, I have learned a little bit about data management, customer relations, and computer programming. These competencies, however, are secondary to the lessons of humility, responsibility, and gratitude learned from my family.
